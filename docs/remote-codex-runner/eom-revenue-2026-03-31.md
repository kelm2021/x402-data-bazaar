# Goal

## Operating Mode

- Operate autonomously from the remote server.
- Work primarily in `~/work/x402-data-bazaar`.
- You may create new sibling repos under `~/work` when that materially improves revenue odds.
- You may create code, docs, scripts, tests, deploys, GTM assets, direct-service offers, and new x402 sellers without waiting for approval.
- x402 is one rail among several, not the identity of the business.
- Keep the business OS artifacts current under `~/ops/business-os` when that folder exists.
- Default to silent execution: keep building, verifying, and updating artifacts without pausing for user-facing narration unless blocked or explicitly asked.

## Guardrails

- Do not use destructive git commands such as `git reset --hard`.
- Do not remove or overwrite unrelated user work.
- Prefer believable cash and buyer-intent distribution surfaces over low-signal catalog sprawl.
- If a live paid verification would exceed available wallet balance or the allowed spend cap, note the blocker and move on.
- Prefer MCP-ready distribution surfaces, direct buyer workflows, and direct operator sales over theory-heavy exploration.
- Interrupt only for destructive decisions, unusually risky moves, or true external blockers.
- Count channel wins only when the latest evidence receipt records an external event such as a reply, workflow request, non-self probe, or paid attempt.

## Revenue Priorities

- Keep `restricted-party-screen` as the current lead wedge until evidence disproves it.
- Keep `vendor-entity-brief` as the bundle / upsell, not the lead.
- Improve existing high-signal sellers before expanding low-signal ones.
- Treat direct workflow services and productized outside-Bazaar offers as valid bridge paths to cash if the current Bazaar route does not produce non-self demand quickly.
