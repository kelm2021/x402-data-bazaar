# Outside-Bazaar Offer Concepts

Based on the current compliance/trust wedge. These are concrete enough to pitch this week without depending on Bazaar discovery.

## 1. Productized Service: Counterparty Trust Sprint

**Who it is for:** procurement, ops, and compliance teams that need to clear a new vendor or partner fast.

**What it is:** a fixed-scope, same-day trust package for one counterparty. It includes a restricted-party screen, a short risk summary, and a plain-English recommendation: proceed, review, or stop.

**Why buyers would care:** it removes the “we need to figure out who can do this” delay and gives the team a usable decision artifact in one pass.

**Pitch this week:** “Send me one vendor name and I’ll give you a same-day trust packet you can hand to procurement or compliance.”

**Believable pricing:** low hundreds per case, or a small flat fee for a single entity. Keep it simple and easy to approve.

## 2. Done-With-You / Concierge Offer: Vendor Onboarding Desk

**Who it is for:** small teams that do not have a dedicated vendor-risk or onboarding function.

**What it is:** a hands-on service where we sit with the team for one onboarding cycle, screen the counterparty, draft the vendor note, and help them decide what to ask for next.

**Why buyers would care:** they get a working process, not just a one-off result. This is useful when the team is stuck between legal, procurement, and operations.

**Pitch this week:** “I’ll help you clear one vendor end-to-end so your team has a repeatable onboarding flow instead of a one-off scramble.”

**Believable pricing:** a modest project fee for the cycle, with a clear stop point after the first live use.

## 3. Recurring Monitoring Offer: Watchlist Maintenance

**Who it is for:** teams that repeatedly deal with the same counterparties, vendors, payout recipients, or partners.

**What it is:** a recurring monitoring service that watches named entities and flags changes, matches, or follow-up actions. It can include a weekly summary and an escalation note when something changes.

**Why buyers would care:** they do not want to re-run the same checks manually every time. They want a lightweight alerting layer that keeps the trust decision fresh.

**Pitch this week:** “Give me your top counterparties and I’ll keep an eye on them so you do not have to re-check from scratch every week.”

**Believable pricing:** monthly subscription pricing tied to a small watchlist size, with a clear cap on how many entities are included.

## Positioning Notes

- Lead with trust, speed, and handoff quality.
- Sell the outcome, not the underlying screen.
- Keep the first offer fixed-scope so it is easy to buy.
- Use the concierge offer when the buyer wants help, not just output.
- Use the monitoring offer when the pain is recurring and manual review keeps coming back.
