# First 14-Day Proof Plan

## Goal

Determine, within 14 days, whether the current lead wedge can credibly produce revenue fast enough to support the `$1,000` net goal by `2026-03-31`.

## Lead Wedge

- `restricted-party-screen` is the lead wedge.
- `vendor-entity-brief` is the bundle / upsell.
- Bazaar-first is the default route unless the evidence says the market fit is weak.

## What Each Row Means

- **Bazaar Probes:** outside actors who tested the lead wedge through Bazaar or a Bazaar-adjacent flow.
- **Paid Attempts:** non-self payment attempts, successful or failed, that show buying intent.
- **Direct Outreach Responses:** replies from direct buyer outreach that ask for details, a sample, pricing, or a demo.
- **Bundle Interest:** requests for `vendor-entity-brief` or any follow-on bundle after the screen is introduced.
- **Self Activity:** local tests, operator QA, and anything clearly initiated by the workspace owner.
- **External Activity:** outside probes, replies, retries, or paid attempts from non-self actors.
- **Telemetry Trust:** whether the day’s data is clean enough to trust.
- **Decision:** keep Bazaar-first, tighten packaging, or pivot to direct-service-first.

## Probe Definition

A probe is any outside action that shows another operator or buyer is testing the offer.

Count as a probe:

- a request that reaches the service from a source that is not the workspace owner
- a buyer asking for the screen, pricing, or a sample flow
- a retry after a 402 response
- a direct question about using the wedge in a real workflow
- a response from outreach that asks for details, sample output, or a demo

Do not count as a probe:

- local testing
- self-generated calls
- repeated page refreshes from the same operator
- internal QA without a distinct outside actor
- vague engagement that does not ask for the service

## Traction Definition

Traction means the wedge is not just noticed, but is being used or seriously considered.

Count as traction:

- at least one non-self probe
- repeat probe behavior from the same outside actor
- a paid call or payment attempt that is not from the workspace owner
- a request to use the bundle after the lead wedge is introduced
- a direct buyer workflow that makes the service easier to continue using

Do not count as traction:

- likes, views, or broad social attention without follow-through
- self-testing that looks like demand
- one-off curiosity with no request to continue

## Self Versus External Data

### Self Data

Self data includes:

- local shell tests
- manual checks from the operating machine
- internal smoke tests
- any call clearly initiated by the operator team

Self data is useful for correctness, but it is not evidence of market demand.

### External Data

External data includes:

- outside probes
- paid calls from non-self actors
- messages from real buyers
- repeated usage from the same outside actor
- downstream action caused by a direct outreach attempt

External data is the only thing that can prove the lead wedge is pulling demand.

## Checkpoints

### Day 3

Ask:

- Is the offer pack clear enough that an outside actor could understand what to buy?
- Do we have any non-self probes?
- Is the telemetry clean enough to tell self from external activity?

Decision:

- If no, tighten packaging and telemetry before adding more wedges.

### Day 7

Ask:

- Has Bazaar-first produced at least one external probe?
- Has the bundle or upsell been requested by anyone outside the workspace?
- Are buyers reacting to price, trust, or discovery?

Decision:

- If there are zero external probes, Bazaar-first is weak and direct-service-first becomes the primary path.

### Day 10

Ask:

- Did any outside actor retry, pay, or ask for a follow-on step?
- Did direct buyer outreach produce a response that sounds like real intent?
- Is the lead wedge still more believable than an outside-service offer?

Decision:

- If the wedge has attention but no conversion, reduce friction or lower price before building more.

### Day 14

Ask:

- Can the current Bazaar path plausibly contribute to the `$1,000` goal before month end?
- Is there enough external evidence to justify staying Bazaar-first?
- Would a direct-service-first offer get to cash faster?

Decision:

- If there are still no meaningful external probes or paid signals, pivot from Bazaar-first to direct-service-first.

## Pivot Thresholds

Pivot from Bazaar-first to direct-service-first if any of the following happen:

- zero non-self probes after focused distribution
- probes appear but conversion stays near zero after one packaging fix
- the offer is understandable but not compelling enough to trigger a response
- the current price is believable but still not enough to create action
- direct-service outreach looks more credible and faster than the marketplace route

Stay Bazaar-first if all of the following remain true:

- outside probes are appearing
- the wedge is understandable without custom explanation
- paid intent is at least plausible
- the bundle path is helping rather than distracting

## End-Of-Day Rule

Do not confuse activity with traction.

Only external behavior that shows buyer intent, paid intent, or repeat usage should keep the current wedge alive.
