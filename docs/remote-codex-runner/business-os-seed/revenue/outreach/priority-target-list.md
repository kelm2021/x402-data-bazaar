# Priority Target List (Service Sales)

Primary offer: `Revenue Ops Build Week` (`$1,200`)  
Fast-entry offer: `Agent OS Setup Sprint` (`$600`)

## Goal

Build a 40-contact list of buyers who can approve a fixed-fee implementation this month.

## Target Segments

### Segment A: AI Founders

Who to contact:

- founder
- COO
- head of operations

Why it fits:

- they feel execution bottlenecks directly
- they can approve small fixed-fee engagements quickly

### Segment B: Agency Owners

Who to contact:

- founder
- managing partner
- delivery lead

Why it fits:

- they need repeatable delivery systems
- they can apply the operating setup across clients

### Segment C: Solo Operators / Consultants

Who to contact:

- independent consultant
- fractional operator
- one-person agency owner

Why it fits:

- they need throughput and follow-up discipline
- implementation value is immediate and visible

## List Shape For This Week

- 15 AI founders
- 15 agency owners
- 10 solo operators

For each contact capture:

- name
- role
- firm
- public contact path
- one-line bottleneck hypothesis
- best-fit offer (`$600` sprint or `$1,200` build week)

## Qualification Rules

A target is in scope if all are true:

- public role suggests authority over execution or operations
- clear signs of active shipping or client delivery
- reachable public channel exists

Out of scope:

- no public channel
- no execution ownership
- obvious enterprise-only procurement path with long cycle

## Search Patterns

- `"AI founder" operations bottleneck`
- `"agency owner" client delivery process`
- `"fractional operator" lead follow-up`
- `"consulting founder" workflow automation`
- `site:linkedin.com/in ("founder" OR "owner") ("AI" OR "agency") ("operations" OR "delivery")`

## Outreach Rules

- one fixed-fee offer per message
- ask for function + bottleneck + next-week outcome
- no tool pitch, no feature list, no endpoint language
- move responders to scope discussion fast
