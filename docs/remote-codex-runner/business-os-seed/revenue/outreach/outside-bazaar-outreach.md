# Outside-Bazaar Outreach Plan

Lead wedge: `restricted-party-screen`
Bundle / upsell: `vendor-entity-brief`

Goal: find money outside Bazaar by selling the lead wedge into real buyer workflows that already exist, then expanding to the brief only when it reduces review time.

## Money Angle 1: Fractional Procurement And Vendor Onboarding Operators

Who to contact:
- fractional procurement leads
- vendor onboarding consultants
- operations consultants who run approval workflows for SMBs and mid-market teams
- founders or operators who own supplier intake

Why this angle fits:
- they already live inside the exact buyer workflow we want
- they can introduce the screen as a default gate in an existing process
- they are easier to reach than large enterprises and can move fast

Quickest target list shape:
- 15 to 25 people
- mix of LinkedIn and direct email
- prioritize people who mention procurement, vendor onboarding, AP, or ops in their title or recent posts
- target consultants with visible client work, not generic agency pages

Opening message:
`I am working on a very small counterparty screen for procurement and vendor onboarding workflows. It is meant to be the first cheap gate before a human spends time on a full review. If that fits any of your client workflows, I can send a 30-second example and the copy-paste prompt this week.`

Follow-up ask:
- ask whether they have a client who approves new suppliers or vendors every week
- offer to tailor the prompt to their approval flow

## Money Angle 2: Compliance, Risk, And Due Diligence Advisors

Who to contact:
- boutique compliance consultants
- AML/KYC advisors
- risk and vendor due diligence firms
- fractional compliance operators

Why this angle fits:
- they already sell judgment and review time
- the bundle can become their handoff artifact
- the screen can be positioned as a low-friction first pass before escalation

Quickest target list shape:
- 15 to 20 people
- find practitioners posting about onboarding, due diligence, sanctions, counterparty risk, or supplier screening
- prefer independent operators and small firms that can reply quickly
- include 3 to 5 people who consult for payments, fintech, or marketplaces

Opening message:
`We built a small counterparty screen that gives a fast first-pass answer before a human does deeper due diligence. It is meant to save review time, not replace judgment. If you work with teams that screen vendors, payees, or counterparties, I can show you the screen plus the short brief add-on this week.`

Follow-up ask:
- ask if they ever need a lightweight screen before escalation
- ask if a short entity brief would help them hand off a case faster

## Money Angle 3: AP, Finance Ops, And Payout Approval Operators

Who to contact:
- AP consultants
- finance operations freelancers
- fractional CFOs
- bookkeeping or back-office operators who approve new payees or contractors

Why this angle fits:
- they already care about who gets paid and when
- they can use the screen as a pre-payment gate
- the brief is useful when someone needs a human-readable summary for approval

Quickest target list shape:
- 20 people
- mix of LinkedIn, email, and warm intros
- prioritize operators who mention AP automation, vendor setup, payout workflows, or finance ops
- include agencies serving marketplaces, creator platforms, or contractor-heavy businesses

Opening message:
`I am reaching out because we have a cheap first-pass screen for new counterparties before payout or vendor setup. It is designed for finance ops and AP workflows where someone wants a quick yes/no gate before a human review. If that is relevant, I can send a copy-paste prompt or a live example this week.`

Follow-up ask:
- ask whether they already have a manual step for new payees or vendors
- offer a short bundle version when they need a handoff note

## Outreach Rules For This Week

- Keep the target list small and specific.
- Contact real operators, not broad marketing inboxes.
- Lead with the screen, not the bundle.
- Offer the brief only when it saves review time or creates a handoff artifact.
- Track whether the first reply is interest in the screen, the bundle, or a custom workflow.
- If a target asks for a broader compliance or onboarding package, treat that as a signal to pitch the bundle.

## Simple Conversion Signals

- reply asking for a demo or prompt
- request for pricing
- request to try it on one real vendor or payee
- request to share with a teammate
- request for the brief after the screen

## This Week's Execution

1. Build a 15 to 25 person list for each angle.
2. Send the three opening messages in batches of 5 to 10.
3. Watch which angle gets the first concrete reply.
4. Push the bundle only after the screen gets interest.
5. Log the best-performing angle in the revenue pipeline.
