# Franklin Later Bridge Memo

## Mission

Keep today's lead wedge converting while exposing later rails only as evidence-backed options. Franklin owns the bridge, not the product line.

## What Franklin can update

- Bridge order and which rail is primary, secondary, or watch-only.
- Packaging emphasis between direct lead wedge, direct-service-first, and bundle-led workflow.
- Surface priority across `Payments MCP`, `MCP` wrappers, app directories, `ACP`, and `Moltbook`.
- Which rails get promoted only after real buyer evidence, not after a theory shift.

## Current bridge order

1. Bazaar/direct lead wedge through `x402` and direct buyer workflows.
2. `Payments MCP` as the buyer shell and low-friction invocation layer.
3. `MCP` wrappers plus discovery surfaces like the `MCP Registry`, `Smithery`, and `xgate`.
4. ChatGPT app-directory surfaces when in-chat UI or broader tool discovery improves conversion.
5. `ACP` only when the work is clearly multi-step, stateful, escrowed, or reputation-based.
6. `Moltbook` only as a signal surface, not a core rail.

## Evidence that matters

- 3+ non-self probes in 7 days.
- 1+ paid conversion, or 2+ serious buyer conversations that reach a closeable state.
- Direct-service closes faster than self-serve for the same buyer.
- Bundle attach rate rises because buyers ask for a handoff artifact.
- A newer rail lowers CAC, increases conversion, or raises average revenue per buyer for 2 to 4 cycles.

## How to keep future rails visible

- Keep one canonical backend and one offer truth.
- Keep future rails labeled `watch`, `secondary`, or `promote`, not all active by default.
- Add only thin wrappers and manifests per rail until evidence justifies more.
- Treat `Payments MCP`, app directories, `ACP`, and `Moltbook` as visible options, not separate businesses.

## Switch thresholds

- Stay Bazaar/direct if the screen keeps getting non-self probes and the buyer does not ask for a reviewer-ready artifact.
- Move to direct-service-first if 2 or more serious buyer conversations close faster through human-assisted screening than through product flow.
- Move to bundle-led workflow if 2 of the last 4 serious opportunities ask for context, explanation, or a handoff pack.
- Promote `Payments MCP` only if it reduces drop-off versus direct asks.
- Promote `MCP` wrappers and app directories only if they outperform direct `x402` on conversion or ease of use.
- Promote `ACP` only when the work is job-shaped enough that state, escrow, or completion-based payout is the better fit.

## Anti-patterns

- Building separate products for each rail.
- Promoting a rail on vibes, social noise, or dashboard confusion.
- Adding `ACP` before the workflow is actually job-shaped.
- Treating `Moltbook` chatter as demand without downstream action.
- Overpricing the screen before the buyer proves ROI.
- Broadening distribution before one path has won for 2 to 4 cycles.
