# Carver Now Memo

## Mission

Keep the current-market truth tight enough to choose the next revenue move without pretending the wedge is proven.

## Live Wedge

- `restricted-party-screen` stays the lead wedge.
- `vendor-entity-brief` stays the bundle / upsell.
- x402 stays the rail, not the identity.
- The current read is still trust/compliance first, generic data later if at all.
- Current signal is weak: 3 sends, 1 Moltbook post, 0 replies. That is not proof, but it is not enough to kill the wedge yet.

## What Carver Can Adjust

- Price within believable bands.
- Framing between lookup, gate, bundle, and workflow step.
- Which buyer objection is being answered first.
- Whether the bundle stays secondary or becomes the lead packaging.
- Whether the next push goes through direct outreach, Payments MCP, or both.

## Evidence To Mine

- Non-self probes.
- Replies that ask for screening, context, or a decision signal.
- Paid calls.
- Repeat use from the same outside source.
- First objection category: price, trust, discovery, or “this is just a lookup.”
- Whether buyers want the screen alone or the screen plus handoff context.

## Price Band Rails

- Simple utility / low-friction gate: `$0.001-$0.01`.
- Core screening: `$0.005-$0.02`.
- Screening plus brief: `$0.02-$0.10` only if it clearly compresses a review step.
- Above `$0.10` only if the offer has become a real workflow product, not a lookup.
- The old `$15/$25` style is wrong for this lane unless the product changes shape materially.
- Do not raise price because the revenue target is high. Raise it only when buyer behavior shows added value.

## Thresholds For Change

- Raise price band only after a buyer asks for more handling, more certainty, or more handoff context.
- Reframe the objection only after the same objection repeats across multiple outside replies.
- Increase wedge confidence only after at least one external reply, one paid probe, or one repeat request proves workflow use.
- Pivot away from the compliance wedge if 7-14 days of focused distribution produce no replies, no paid probes, or only “custom help” requests.

## Fast Falsifiers

- No non-self replies after the next 7-14 day window.
- Replies arrive but only ask for custom manual help, not the gate itself.
- The route only works when priced so low it is effectively a rounding error.
- Another wedge clearly earns real paid demand faster.
- The call is treated as a nice-to-have lookup instead of a workflow gate.

## Anti-Patterns

- Hero pricing on public-data-like output.
- Treating x402 as the business instead of the rail.
- Expanding into new wedges before the live wedge is either validated or falsified.
- Confusing posts, sends, or impressions with demand.
- Letting generic data language replace the trust/compliance buyer job.
